## v2.0 — March 2026 (Current)

**Five architectural additions:**

- **Positioning Layer** — Formal distinction between Position (S, scalar) and Positioning (V, vector). Resolves the most persistent diagnostic blindspot: Nokia's Position was 95/100; Positioning was terminal.
- **Gradient Navigation Theorem** — V_optimal = ∇S. Optimal direction is the gradient of the scalar field — mathematical derivation, not executive judgment.
- **Critical Velocity Theorem** — V_critical = R_static × F_resistance / (S̃_base × Παm). Below this threshold, effort produces zero outcome regardless of magnitude.
- **Slingshot Coefficient (η)** — Quantifies field-as-lever. η = |F| × cos(θ_VF). Introduces Slingshot Reversal as a previously unmodeled failure mode.
- **Phase Dynamics Engine** — Recursive feedback model with τ (field response delay) producing the False Safety Window theorem.

**Bug fixes from peer review:**
- Fix 1: Dimensionless notation S̃, Ṽ, F̃, α̃f ∈ [0, 1] throughout
- Fix 2: Stochastic trajectory declaration added (Section 4.2)
- Fix 3: Time delay τ in phase dynamics F(t+1) = f[S(t+1−τ), ΔExternal]

**Validation:**
- Nokia 2007–2013: Full v2.0 parameter trace with 7-cycle phase dynamics
- Korea 1997: Slingshot Reversal mechanism formally modeled
- Individual learning trajectory: Prospective prediction registered (verify July 2026)
